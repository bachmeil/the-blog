This post is really not a post, it's a comment inspired by [this post by someone leaving lobste.rs](https://akselmo.dev/posts/leaving-lobste-rs-rant/).

I've been using the internet for a long time - long enough to remember the release of Mosaic being a big deal. I've been around for all the major online communities that have formed (obviously never having used the vast majority of them). One of the sad realities of online communities is that eventually they are all ruined by folks posting stuff that isn't the reason the site exists.

I gave up on Hacker News earlier this year. I do still visit the site to read articles. Used in that way, however, Hacker News is just a different version of Google News. I occasionally check the comments on an article, mostly to check for a free link to an article I can't read or whatever. I don't bother reading comments written by others and I don't post comments. I once heard someone say they avoid HN because "there's nothing there" in the comments in the context of running a business. Sadly, I think we've reached that point on HN for all topics now. The comments are low-effort, uninformed, and lazy. The laziness of the responses to the final comment I posted there made me ask "Is this really the best way to spend my remaining years?" That was, as the saying goes, the straw that broke the camel's back. Nothing major by itself, but it clarified that my time there was wasted, so I decided I'm done participating in the comments there. (I'm not claiming anyone else is going to care!)

So now let's bring this back to the post I'm commenting on.

> But then I get someone coaxing me to admit it's a preference and not an accessibility need.

That's where these sites lose their value:

- Someone has no expertise in a topic, but wishes to express strong opinions. There's no depth to their thinking or authoritative sources. Just a bit of surface-level thinking on a topic they know nothing about.
- They do it in a way that is aggressive or confrontational.
- Others with no expertise use the scoring system of the site (upvotes in this case) to endorse the comment.
- Debating the validity of ADHD has nothing to do with the purpose of the site.

So that makes you ask: Should I participate in a site where I have to debate the medical side of ADHD with people that know nothing about the topic? Probably not. That sounds like a boring proposition. And given the scoring system that rewards people for saying things you know are wrong, it's also frustration. You're playing a game that makes no sense, like when you're a kid and you join in a game on the playground and they have the rules all mixed up.
